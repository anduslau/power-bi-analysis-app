### 1. Executive Summary
This document outlines the business requirements and technical specifications for the **Sales Performance Dashboard** (file: `sample.rdl`). The report is a foundational sales analysis tool designed to provide stakeholders with a view of sales orders from the AdventureWorks database. It currently offers a basic tabular view of sales transactions, filtered by a customizable date range. The primary purpose is to enable quick review of sales order details within a specified period. This BRD details the current implementation, its business context, and provides recommendations for enhancement to increase its business value.

### 2. Business Purpose & Context
*   **Primary Objective:** To allow business users (e.g., Sales Managers, Finance Analysts) to monitor and review sales order transactions from the AdventureWorks company.
*   **Business Need:** Provides a centralized, queryable view of sales data to support operational review, answer ad-hoc questions about specific orders, and serve as a base for more detailed sales analysis.
*   **Key Stakeholders:** Sales Operations, Financial Controllers, and Business Analysts who require direct access to transactional sales data.
*   **Context:** This appears to be a foundational or prototype report. It focuses on raw data extraction (order ID, date, and amount due) rather than aggregated performance metrics, suggesting its use is for data verification, detailed investigation, or as a building block for more complex dashboards.

*   **OrderDate:** The date on which the sales order was placed. This is the primary temporal dimension for analysis and filtering.
*   **TotalDue:** The final monetary amount due for the order. This is a critical financial metric representing the revenue value of each transaction.

### 8. Business Rules & Logic
The embedded business logic in this report is straightforward:
1.  **Data Selection Rule:** The report includes **all columns** from the `Sales.SalesOrderHeader` table. A business rule should be established to define which columns are necessary for end-user consumption to avoid information overload.
2.  **Temporal Filtering Rule:** The core business rule is that only sales orders with an `OrderDate` greater than or equal to the user-selected `StartDate` **and** less than or equal to the user-selected `EndDate` are displayed. This isolates analysis to a specific timeframe.

### 10. Glossary of Terms
*   **AdventureWorks:** A sample database from Microsoft, often used for training and demonstration. It simulates a manufacturing and retail company.
*   **Sales.SalesOrderHeader:** The primary database table containing one record for each sales order placed. It holds summary information about the order.
*   **TotalDue:** The total amount of money the customer is required to pay for a specific sales order, inclusive of subtotal, tax, and freight.
*   **RDL (Report Definition Language):** The XML-based file format used by Microsoft SQL Server Reporting Services (SSRS) to define reports.
*   **Tablix:** The flexible data region control in SSRS reports that can behave as a table, matrix (crosstab), or list.
*   **Parameter:** A variable in the report that a user can interact with to dynamically filter or change the data displayed.
